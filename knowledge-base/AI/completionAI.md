### How are off-plan payments protected?
Through escrow accounts:
- All buyer payments go into escrow
- Funds released based on construction progress

Source: https://dlp.dubai.gov.ae/

### What is an escrow account?
A regulated account where:
- Your money is held securely
- Developer cannot freely access funds

### What can escrow funds be used for?
- Construction
- Consultants
- Marketing (max 5%)

### What happens if I default as a buyer?

| Completion Level | Developer Right |
|------------------|----------------|
| ≥80% complete | Keep all payments |
| ≥60% complete | Keep up to 40% |

Source: https://dlp.dubai.gov.ae/

### What protections exist in Abu Dhabi?

- No escrow withdrawals until 20% completion
- Escrow funds protected from seizure
- Buyers receive financial reporting

Source: https://adrec.gov.ae/

### What happens if the developer delays?
In Abu Dhabi:
- If delay > 6 months
- Authorities may impose penalties in favor of buyers

### What happens if the project fails?
Possible outcomes:
- New developer completes project
- Refund via escrow
- Third-party intervention

### Are payment plans standardized?
No. Payment schedules:
- Vary by developer
- Often linked to construction milestones

### Are there cooling-off periods?
Not clearly defined in official sources.

### Do rules differ across emirates?
Yes:
- Dubai and Abu Dhabi are most regulated
- Other emirates have less transparency

### Key takeaway for investors
Your main protections are:
- Escrow accounts
- Regulatory oversight
- Developer compliance enforcement
